# MAVEN-T: Multi-Agent enVironment-aware Enhanced Neural Trajectory predictor with Reinforcement Learning 

Wenchang Duan, Zhenguo Gao, Yaoliang Yu, Yi Shi 

Abstract —Trajectory prediction remains a critical yet chal-lenging component in autonomous driving systems, requiring sophisticated reasoning capabilities while meeting stringent real-time deployment constraints. While knowledge distillation has demonstrated effectiveness in model compression, existing ap-proaches fail to preserve complex decision-making capabilities during distillation, particularly in dynamic multi-agent scenarios. This paper introduces MAVEN-T, a teacher-student framework achieving state-of-the-art trajectory prediction through comple-mentary architectural co-design and progressive distillation. The teacher employs hybrid attention mechanisms for maximum representational capacity, while the student uses efficient archi-tectures optimized for deployment. Knowledge transfer occurs via multi-granular distillation with adaptive curriculum learning that dynamically adjusts complexity based on performance. Critically, the framework incorporates reinforcement learning to break the ”imitation ceiling” of traditional distillation—enabling the student to verify, refine, and optimize teacher knowledge through dynamic environmental interaction, potentially achieving more robust decision-making than the teacher itself. Extensive experiments on NGSIM and highD datasets demonstrate 6.2× parameter compression and 3.7× inference speedup while main-taining state-of-the-art accuracy, establishing a new paradigm for deploying sophisticated reasoning models under resource constraints. 

Index Terms —Trajectory prediction, autonomous driving, knowledge distillation, reinforcement learning, teacher-student framework, multi-agent systems.

II. R ELATED WORK 

A. Knowledge Distillation in Autonomous Driving 

Knowledge distillation for autonomous driving has gained attention for deploying sophisticated models in resource-constrained environments. Chen et al. [1] proposed S2TNet with transformer architectures but lacks efficient knowledge transfer for real-time deployment. Yuan et al. [5] introduced AgentFormer for multi-agent modeling, yet suffers from com-putational inefficiency on edge devices. Liu et al. [2] developed mmTransformer for multimodal prediction but fails to preserve complex reasoning in lightweight models. Huang et al. [6] highlighted the gap between sophisticated modeling and deployment constraints. Zhou et al. [7] presented HiVT with hierarchical attention but lacks progressive knowl-edge transfer strategies. Shi et al. [8] demonstrated advanced MTR capabilities but does not address efficient model com-pression without sacrificing decision-making quality. 

B. Graph Neural Networks for Vehicle Interaction Modeling 

Graph neural networks show promise for modeling vehicle interactions but suffer from computational limitations. Liang et al. [9] introduced LaneGCN for actor-map interactions but is computationally intensive for real-time applications. Li et al. [10] proposed GRIP using GCNs but lacks sophisticated attention mechanisms for long-range dependencies. Kosaraju et al. [11] employed Social-BiGAT for trajectory forecasting but struggles with computational efficiency in multi-vehicle scenarios. Salzmann et al. [12] developed Trajec-tron++ achieving strong performance but requiring substantial computational resources. Gilles et al. [13] proposed GOHOME representing HD maps as graphs but lacks efficient knowledge distillation mechanisms. Li et al. [14] extended GRIP++ with dynamic edge weights but still suffers from scalability issues. 

C. Hybrid Attention Mechanisms and Temporal Modeling 

Attention mechanisms for autonomous driving face com-putational complexity challenges. Mercat et al. [15] explored multi-head self-attention but failed to address quadratic com-plexity issues. Giuliari et al. [16] proposed encoder-decoder transformers but lacks linear scalability for extended temporal horizons. Nayakanti et al. [17] introduced Wayformer with multi-axis attention but struggles with computational efficiency. Chen et al. [18] proposed VNAGT combining variational approaches with graph transformers but lacks progressive distillation mechanisms for efficient deployment. 

D. Progressive Learning and Parameter-Efficient Adaptation 

Curriculum learning applications lack adaptive mechanisms for knowledge distillation. Huang et al. [19] introduced Game-Former with level-k reasoning but does not provide adaptive curriculum progression based on student model performance. Parameter-efficient adaptation methods remain limited in autonomous driving contexts. Feng et al. [20] proposed Mac-Former with map-aware attention but lacks low-rank adapta-tion strategies for efficient knowledge transfer. 

E. Multimodal Fusion and Attention Transfer 

Multimodal fusion strategies face attention transfer chal-lenges. Xu et al. [21] developed Tra2Tra with global attention but lacks multi-scale feature alignment for effective knowledge distillation. Li et al. [22] proposed RAIN combining reinforce-ment learning with attention but does not address progressive transfer of attention patterns. Zhou et al. [23] introduced GA-STT for spatial-temporal transformers but lacks sophisticated attention transfer mecha-nisms for preserving teacher model reasoning in lightweight architectures. III. VII. C ONCLUSION 

This paper presents MAVEN-T, a knowledge distillation framework resolving the conflict between reasoning complex-ity and deployment efficiency in autonomous driving. The core principle employs complementary co-design: a power-ful teacher with hybrid attention mechanisms paired with a lightweight GRU-based student. Multi-granular adaptive curriculum distillation, enhanced by reinforcement learning, transfers nuanced decision-making capabilities while breaking the imitation ceiling. MAVEN-T achieves state-of-the-art pre-diction accuracy with 6.2× parameter compression and 3.7× in-ference speedup. The findings validate that distinct, synergistic teacher-student architectures provide an effective pathway for deploying advanced reasoning in resource-constrained, safety-critical systems. In the future, the proposed framework can be further extended toward more adaptive and parameter-efficient learning paradigms [24], [25].
